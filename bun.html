Steamed Red Bean Bun

From Spirited Away (2001)

Tired from a long day of work at the bathhouse or from looking out for your dragon boyfriend? Then chow down on this semi-sweet treat with your good friend!"

INGREDIENTS:

INSTRUCTIONS:

  1. Prepare the red bean paste by either following your favorite recipe (leftovers can be frozen for future use) or using store-bought paste. Ensure it’s completely cooled before using.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, dissolve 1 teaspoon of sugar in 1/4 cup of lukewarm water. Add the active dry yeast, stir, and let it sit for about 15 minutes until it becomes foamy.
  3. Stir lukewarm milk into the yeast mixture, then add the flour, powdered sugar, and a pinch of salt. Knead the dough by hand or with a dough hook until it is soft, smooth, and no longer sticky.
  4. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.
  5. Prepare six 3” x 3” squares of parchment paper for the buns to sit on during steaming.
  6. Roll the red bean paste into six balls, approximately 35g each, and cover them with a damp towel to keep them from drying out.
  7. Divide the dough into six equal pieces. Roll each piece into a disc, place a red bean paste ball in the center, and wrap the dough around the filling, pinching the edges to seal.
  8. Set up your steamer with about 5 cups of warm water. Place each bun, seam-side down, on a parchment square in the steamer. Leave at least 1 inch of space between buns. Let them proof for 30 minutes inside the steamer without turning on the heat.
  9. After proofing, turn the heat to medium-high and steam the buns for 15 minutes with the lid tightly closed. Do not open the lid during this time.
  10. Turn off the heat and let the buns sit in the steamer for an additional 5 minutes with the lid still covered. Afterward, carefully remove the lid, and your steamed red bean buns are ready to enjoy!
