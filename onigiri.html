Haku's Onigiri

From Spirited Away (2001)

A rice snack to give to your troubled friend who's parents got turned into pigs!

INGREDIENTS:

INSTRUCTIONS:

  1. Start by rinsing 1 cup of sushi rice thoroughly (mochi rice can also be used, but sushi rice is recommended). Cook the rice using a rice cooker or on the stovetop.
  2. If cooking on the stovetop, combine the rinsed rice with 2 cups of water in a pot. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for 15 minutes without lifting the lid.
  3. After 15 minutes, turn off the heat and let the rice rest, covered, for another 2-3 minutes to finish cooking. Once done, allow the rice to cool slightly before forming the rice balls.
  4. In a bowl, combine the tuna, Kewpie mayo, sesame oil, soy sauce (or salt), and optional spicy sauce. Mix well, tasting and adjusting the seasonings until the flavors are balanced.
  5. Place a sheet of plastic wrap on your work surface. Scoop a portion of rice onto the plastic wrap and shape it into a triangle. Add a generous spoonful of the tuna mixture to the center of the rice triangle, then cover it with more rice to seal the filling.
  6. Using the plastic wrap, gently mold the rice into a ball, ensuring the filling is fully enclosed. Twist the plastic wrap to shape the rice ball and prevent sticking. Repeat until all the rice and filling are used, making 5-7 rice balls.
  7. For best results, refrigerate the rice balls briefly to help them firm up.
  8. Optional: Drizzle rice vinegar over the rice balls for a touch of extra flavor. Enjoy!
